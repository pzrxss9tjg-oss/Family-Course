When we say a family is dysfunctional, we mean the system repeatedly fails at the tasks a healthy family should do: create safety, allow emotions, manage conflict, set appropriate boundaries, and form identities that are not controlled by fear or role expectations.

This module‚Äôs slide outline highlights key signals to listen for: The Scapegoat ‚Äì Acts out, blamed, often misunderstood The Lost Child ‚Äì Invisible, withdrawn, self-sufficient The Mascot ‚Äì Uses humor to deflect pain The Caretaker ‚Äì Parentified, emotionally responsible for others üëâ Ask clergy: Which role shows up most in your counseling office?.

A useful analytic framework for learners is: (1) identify the pattern, (2) ask what the pattern protects, (3) name the cost (spiritual, relational, emotional), and (4) choose the smallest safe step toward truth and connection. This keeps pastoral care grounded, compassionate, and practical.

Two lanes guide our work throughout the course. Clinical: nervous-system safety, trauma responses, boundaries, and referral when the need exceeds pastoral scope. Biblical: imago Dei, truth spoken in love, repentance and repair, and the distinction between forgiveness and ongoing access.
