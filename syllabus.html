How to use this course

This is a scripture-centered, trauma-aware learning space designed for seminary and ministry formation. It is written for learners, and it can be shared directly with a class. Each module is intentionally broken into short sections. Read one section at a time, pause, and apply.

Last updated: February 4, 2026 Begin Module 01

Learning outcomes

By the end, learners can…

  • Identify dysfunctional family patterns without “diagnosing” people.
  • Distinguish functional vs. dysfunctional systems and communication styles.
  • Recognize survival roles and how they show up in adulthood and ministry.
  • Explain how dysfunction shapes adult faith and the image of God.
  • Apply boundaries and pastoral interventions; differentiate forgiveness from access.
  • Recognize high‑risk disclosures and make appropriate referrals.

Recommended pacing

Choose your track

Course norms

Safety and wise practice

  • We name patterns without shaming people.
  • We avoid graphic disclosure in group settings; we focus on patterns and impacts.
  • We do not pressure survivors into unsafe reconciliation.
  • We treat referral as wisdom, not failure.
  • We use Scripture to support truth and safety—not to minimize harm.

How to use this with a group

Simple expectations

  • Use your own words for reflections and discussion posts.
  • If you quote outside sources, cite them.
  • Protect confidentiality: remove names and identifying details from examples.
